About Me


I am a Ph.D. candidate working in the GATR Research Group in the Earth and Environmental Science department at Tulane University. Currently my research focuses on use of geodetic tools (i.e. SAR, LiDAR, GNSS data) to estimate subsidence in the Gulf of Mexico during the last decades. I am interested in learning open software to use these datasets to quantify surface processes and find out how they relate with human activities worldwide.
